From google maps, the peak is 8 degrees east of south.

Getting the bearing we have, in general

\[\beta = \arctan(X/Y), \quad\quad X = \cos\theta_b\,\sin\Delta L, \quad\quad Y = \cos\theta_a \sin\theta_b - \sin\theta_a \cos\theta_b \cos\Delta L\]

where \(\beta\) is the bearing between the two points.

Limit of close points¶

If two points are close, we can assume $Delta L$ is small. If they are far from the poles, we also have small \(\Delta \theta\). In radians

\[X \approx \Delta L\,\, \cos\theta_b\]

The expression for Y is trickier

\[Y \approx \sin\theta_b\cos\theta_a - \sin(\theta_a)\cos_\theta_b(1 - \Delta L^2/2) = \sin(\theta_b - \theta_a) + \sin(\theta_a) \cos(\theta_b)(\Delta L)^2/2 X/Y \approx \frac{\cos\theta_b}{\sin(\theta_b-\theta_a) + \sin(\theta_a) \cos(\theta_b) (\Delta L)^2/2} \Delta L= \frac{2\cos\theta_b}{2\frac{\Delta \theta}{\Delta L} + \sin\theta_a\cos\theta_b\,\Delta L}\]

Near In the limit of small \(\Delta L\), we have \(X \approx \cos\theta_b \,\Delta L\) and \(Y\approx \cos\theta_a \sing\theta_b - \sin\theta_a\cos\theta_b = sin(\theta_b - \theta_a)\). Therefore

\[\beta \approx \arctan(\Delta L \cos\theta_b/\sin(\theta_b - \theta_a))\]
\[X = cos(174.04817853760065^\circ) sin(0.17578^\circ) = (-0.9946)(0.00306797) = -0.00305143 Y \approx sin(\theta_b - \theta_a) = \sin(-0.027048^\circ) = -0.00047207 \beta = \arctan(6.4638)\]
